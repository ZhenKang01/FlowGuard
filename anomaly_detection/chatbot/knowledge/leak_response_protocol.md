# Leak Response Protocol

## Initial Detection

When a water leak is detected by a sensor or reported by staff:

1. **Assess severity immediately.** Check the sensor reading or visual inspection.
   - Minor drip (< 5 gal/hr): log a work order, schedule repair within 24 hours.
   - Moderate leak (5–50 gal/hr): dispatch a technician within 2 hours, notify the facility manager.
   - Major leak (> 50 gal/hr or structural risk): isolate the water supply immediately, evacuate the area if needed, notify facility manager and call emergency maintenance.

2. **Do not attempt to repair electrical fixtures near water.** Call a licensed electrician.

3. **Contain the leak where safe.** Use absorbent mats or buckets to limit water spread while awaiting repair.

## Escalation Path

| Severity | First Contact | Time to Respond |
|---|---|---|
| Minor | On-call technician | 24 hours |
| Moderate | Facility manager + technician | 2 hours |
| Major | Facility manager + emergency maintenance + building manager | Immediate |

## Valve Isolation

If the leak source is identified and the water must be isolated:
- Technicians may close **zone valves** (serving one room or floor) without additional authorisation.
- **Building main valves** require approval from the facility manager before closing. See *Valve Shutoff Policy*.
- After isolation, post a notice on all affected taps/outlets indicating water is temporarily off.

## Documentation

All leak incidents must be logged as a work order within the FlowGuard system within 1 hour of detection, even if the issue is minor or self-resolved. Include:
- Location (building, floor, room)
- Estimated flow rate or description
- Actions taken
- Name of person who reported it

## Return to Service

After repair, a technician must:
1. Re-open valves slowly and check for secondary leaks.
2. Run taps for 30 seconds to flush the line.
3. Update the work order status to "Resolved" and note the repair method.
