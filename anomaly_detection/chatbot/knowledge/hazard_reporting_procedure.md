# Hazard Reporting Procedure

## What Counts as a Hazard

Any condition that poses a risk to health, safety, or property must be reported. Examples include:
- Exposed wiring or electrical faults near water
- Slippery floors from leaks or cleaning spills
- Blocked fire exits or safety equipment obstructions
- Mould or biological contamination from water damage
- Chemical spills (cleaning agents, boiler chemicals)
- Structural damage (cracked pipes, collapsed ceiling tiles, damp walls)
- Pest infestation linked to water ingress

## Reporting Steps

### Immediate Action (first 5 minutes)
1. Ensure your own safety first. Do not approach if there is electrical or structural risk.
2. If immediate danger: call building security or emergency services (999 / campus security hotline).
3. Alert people nearby to the hazard zone.
4. If safe to do so, place warning signs or tape off the area.

### Formal Report (within 30 minutes)
1. Log a work order in FlowGuard with:
   - **Type**: Hazard Report
   - **Location**: building, floor, room number
   - **Description**: what you saw, any actions already taken
   - **Severity**: choose Critical if immediate danger; High if risk is present but contained; Medium for developing issues
2. Notify the facility manager directly (phone or in person) for Critical or High severity.
3. Take photos where safe and attach them to the work order if possible.

## Who to Notify

| Hazard Type | Primary Contact | Escalate To |
|---|---|---|
| Water / structural | On-call technician + Facility manager | Building manager |
| Electrical | Facility manager | Licensed electrician (do not attempt repair) |
| Chemical spill | Facility manager | MSDS-trained staff, external contractor |
| Fire hazard | Security + Facility manager | Fire safety officer |
| Biological (mould, sewage) | Facility manager | Certified remediation contractor |

## Record Keeping

All hazard reports must be retained for a minimum of 3 years. The work order system generates an audit log automatically when a work order is created. Do not close a hazard work order without confirming the hazard has been remediated and signed off by the facility manager.

## Near-Miss Reporting

Near-miss incidents (no injury, but could have caused harm) must also be reported following the same procedure. Near-miss data is reviewed monthly to prevent future incidents.
