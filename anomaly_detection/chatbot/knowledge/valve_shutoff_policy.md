# Valve Shutoff Policy

## Purpose

This policy governs who may close water valves and under what circumstances, to prevent accidental water outages while ensuring rapid response to leaks.

## Valve Types and Authorisation

| Valve Type | Description | Who May Close | Authorisation Required |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fixture valve | Isolates a single tap, toilet, or appliance | Any technician | None — self-authorised |
| Zone valve | Serves one room or floor (typically 1–20 outlets) | Technician | Verbal approval from facility manager |
| Riser valve | Serves one full building column or wing | Senior technician or facility manager | Written work order; notify building manager |
| Building main valve | Cuts water to the entire building | Facility manager only | Must notify building manager + occupants at least 1 hour in advance |
| Campus/Site main | Cuts water to multiple buildings | Facility director | Emergency only; requires director approval + local utility notification |

## Emergency Shutoff

In an emergency (major burst pipe, flooding, risk to life or property):

1. Any person may close a zone or building main valve to prevent immediate harm.
2. **Notify the facility manager immediately after** — within 5 minutes.
3. Log a work order in FlowGuard within 15 minutes of the emergency shutoff.
4. Post notices on all affected water outlets and doors.
5. Arrange alternate water access (bottled water, temporary standpipe) if the outage will last more than 2 hours.

## Pre-Planned Shutoffs

For scheduled maintenance that requires a shutoff:

1. Submit a work order at least **48 hours in advance** for zone valves, **5 business days** for building main.
2. Facility manager approves and notifies affected occupants via email.
3. Schedule shutoffs outside peak hours (typically 7 AM–6 PM) where possible.
4. Minimum notice to occupants: 24 hours for zone, 48 hours for building main.

## After Reopening a Valve

1. Open slowly over 30 seconds to avoid water hammer (pressure surge).
2. Check for leaks at joints downstream for 5 minutes after opening.
3. Run taps for 30 seconds to flush air and sediment.
4. Confirm with facility manager that service has been restored.
5. Update the work order to "Resolved".

## Documentation

Every valve operation (planned or emergency) must be recorded in a work order with:
- Valve identifier or description
- Date and time opened / closed
- Person who operated the valve
- Reason for operation
- Time water service was restored
