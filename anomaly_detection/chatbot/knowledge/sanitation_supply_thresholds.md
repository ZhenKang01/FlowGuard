# Sanitation Supply Thresholds and Reorder Policy

## Minimum Stock Levels

The following minimum stock levels must be maintained at all times across all facilities. When stock falls to or below the reorder threshold, a work order must be raised immediately.

| Supply Item | Unit | Minimum Stock | Reorder Threshold | Standard Order Quantity |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Soap Dispensers (refill cartridges) | units | 20 | 50 | 100 |
| Toilet Paper | rolls | 100 | 200 | 500 |
| Hand Sanitizer (500 mL bottles) | units | 10 | 30 | 60 |
| Cleaning Chemicals (disinfectant concentrate) | litres | 5 | 10 | 25 |
| Paper Towels | rolls | 100 | 300 | 600 |
| Disposable Gloves (box of 100) | boxes | 3 | 6 | 12 |
| Mop Heads | units | 4 | 8 | 16 |
| Bin Liners (pack of 50) | packs | 5 | 10 | 20 |

## Inspection Frequency

- **Daily**: Check high-traffic restrooms (main lobby, cafeteria, gymnasium).
- **Weekly**: Check all other restrooms, store rooms, and cleaning cupboards.
- **Monthly**: Full inventory audit logged in the FlowGuard system.

## Storage Requirements

- Cleaning chemicals must be stored in a locked, ventilated room away from food preparation areas.
- Flammable cleaning agents must be stored in a fire-rated cabinet.
- Hand sanitizer with > 60% alcohol content is classified as a hazardous material — store away from heat sources.
- First-in, first-out (FIFO) stock rotation must be applied to all consumables.

## Reorder Process

1. Staff member identifies stock below reorder threshold.
2. Log a work order in FlowGuard with: item name, current quantity, building/location.
3. Facility manager approves orders above SGD $200 per line item.
4. Preferred supplier list is available from the facility manager.

## Critical Shortage

If hand sanitizer, soap, or toilet paper falls to zero in any occupied building, it constitutes a critical shortage. The facility manager must be notified immediately and a replacement source identified within 4 hours.
